# Vietnamese Scriptwriting Rules

## Script Structure
- **Hook**: First scene grabs attention (question, bold statement, surprising fact)
- **Body**: 2-5 scenes developing the narrative
- **CTA**: Final scene with call to action or powerful conclusion

## Vietnamese Text Guidelines
- displayText: max 15-20 words per scene, concise and impactful
- Use simple, conversational Vietnamese (avoid formal/academic)
- Each scene's text should be self-contained
- Active voice, present tense

## spokenText vs displayText
- `displayText`: Subtitles shown on screen
- `spokenText`: Text for TTS engine (may differ for pronunciation)
- Common adjustments:
  - Abbreviations → full words: "AI" → "Ây Ai"
  - Add pronunciation hints for English loanwords
  - Commas for breathing pauses, periods for medium pauses
  - Numbers written as words: "100" → "một trăm"

## Scene Pacing
| Type | Scenes | Duration/scene | Total |
|------|--------|---------------|-------|
| Short news | 3-4 | ~3s | 9-12s |
| Educational | 5-6 | ~4s | 20-24s |
| Deep dive | 6-7 | ~4-5s | 24-35s |

## Tone
- Engaging, energetic but natural
- Informative without being preachy
- End with curiosity or call to action
